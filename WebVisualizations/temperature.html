Latitude vs. Temperature

This page explores the relationship between latitude and temperature. Data were pulled from the OpenWeatherMap API for 589 cities on 20 June 2021. Matplotlib was used for the data visualization.

This plot has a curvilinear relationship with locations closer to the equator having generally higher temperatures than than locations further from the equator. Visually, it appears the Southern Hemisphere (i.e., locations with negative latitudes) may have less variability in this relationship than in the Northern Hemisphere, but this could be an artifact of there being more land mass in the Northern Hemisphere and thus more possible cities.
